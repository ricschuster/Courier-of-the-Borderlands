## Next actions (narrative spine, for the fresh session)

Goal: produce a storyline design doc (suggest `docs/design/04_storyline.md`)
that locks the narrative spine so later missions and dialogue have something to
hang on. This is design and content work, not engine work.

1. Draft the frontier premise: one or two paragraphs on what is actually
   happening in the borderland (why roads fail, why settlements go quiet, what
   the player-courier is becoming part of). Keep it mysterious and slightly
   dangerous, per the CLAUDE.md tone default.
2. Define a main thread and a per-region thread (Greybridge, Saltreach,
   Fenmarch), each resolvable through key deliveries plus reputation, with the
   regions ordered into an arc rather than parallel spokes.
3. Say how the spine is delivered: journal entries (`journal.ts` exists), NPC
   dialogue (M5.2, not built yet), and mission chains (M5.3). Keep it emergent
   and place-driven per pillar 4, not a scripted cutscene story.
4. Tie the spine to the north star: which deliveries change world-state, and how
   the world visibly reacting is itself the story.
5. Keep it light. Sketch beats and hooks, not a novel. Do not invent lore that
   contradicts the existing place notes below.

### Existing place-lore to build on (already in the game, keep consistent)

Recurring motifs across all three regions: couriers who vanish, sealed or
unmarked things you are told not to read, places that listen / remember / wait,
old standing stones from a forgotten order, settlements going silent, and one
thing at Thornwick that is "never named."

Greybridge (hub, home Greywater): Greywater (tired river town where every road
begins), Northcairn (standing stones, hill clans trade in silence), Eastwatch
(watchtower always listening, "gone quiet"), Southmill (mill, wheels stopped,
silent for a season), Ironhollow (miners wary of outsiders), Mirewatch
(stilt-village in the marsh, sealed tubes, a stranger with wet boots).

Saltreach (spoke, home Tidewatch): Tidewatch (the tides remember everything),
Reedford (trust nobody who arrives dry), Saltkeep (garrison smaller than the
walls suggest, last courier did not return), Cormorant Rock (birds carry news
faster than any courier, an unsigned letter from someone already gone).

Fenmarch (spoke, home Mossgate): Mossgate (fen mist never lifts before noon),
Duskmere (water turns dark before dusk, parcels the last courier would not
describe), Thornwick (gates barred against something never named, a ledger you
must not read), Hollowfen (old standing stones, "waiting longer than you have
been a courier").
