## Completed

### Batch 1: playtest bug cluster (#131 -> #132)

One root cause behind most of it: region-clear counted every ungated base
contract, but the authored mission steps omitted each region's 4th premium
corner, so the objective under-reported and the region only truly cleared on a
route the mission never named. Since the spoke reveals and the ending gate on
region-clear, that silently blocked progress.

- Added the missing corners (`secret-to-reedgrave`, `saltreach-cipher-to-saltmere`,
  `fenmarch-cipher-for-fenholt`) to their mission steps, plus a regression guard
  that each region's mission contracts must equal its base routes.
- Region-derived the run-summary title and flavour line (was hardcoded
  "Greybridge" for every region).
- `intro-seen` now resets on New Game, so the intro is testable again.
- Postmaster now acknowledges the finished arc (`blockade_broken` greeting).

### Batch 2: travel sink (ADR 0005, #133 -> #134)

The game's first pressure system, so gold/upgrades/skills matter.

- Wagon condition (0-current-max) wears per tile, scaled by raw terrain
  roughness (roads nearly free, mire punishing). Repaired at any settlement for
  gold (R). Relief upgrades and Off-road cut wear through separate, weaker
  floored factors, so a maxed wagon still pays repairs.
- Soft penalty at 0: limp speed plus an optional 50c tow home; never game-over.
- Capacity grows with courier level (RPG-style): starts small and fragile,
  lengthens toward 100 across the arc. No new save state (level is derived);
  only current condition is persisted (additive field, legacy saves load full).
- Difficulty seam: every tunable knob lives in a `WagonTuning` profile with
  relaxed/standard/demanding presets, threaded through one scene field, so a
  future difficulty selector just picks a preset.
- HUD shows current/max and the live repair cost.

### Tuning (playtest-driven, all in the standard profile)

- Wear raised 3x after an instrumented full-arc measurement (~52 -> ~170 points
  of arc wear; the map is small and road-heavy).
- Starting capacity cut 100 -> 40 -> 25 as successive playtests still cleared a
  region without repairing.
- Limp speed cut 0.35x -> 0.15x to close a real exploit: wear floors at 0 and
  repair cost caps at max*rate, so a gentle limp let a player live stranded and
  pay a fixed, known price at leisure. The steep crawl makes a dry wagon a
  problem you fix.

  starting points, not yet balanced.
