## Completed

### Travel-sink measurement tool (opt-in)

`tests/e2e/travel-sink-measure.spec.ts`: the full-arc driver with wagon wear
turned ON (the arc guard runs wear off), instrumented to print the travel-sink
economics. Skipped unless `MEASURE_DIFF` is set, so it never runs in normal
`test:e2e` or CI. Run it with:

```
MEASURE_DIFF=standard  npx playwright test travel-sink-measure --project=chromium
MEASURE_DIFF=demanding npx playwright test travel-sink-measure --project=chromium
```

Caveats baked into the tool's header: the driver takes near-optimal routes (a
wandering player wears MORE) and repairs at every home visit (a diligent
maintainer, the best case for staying healthy). So reported wear is a lower
bound and condition an upper bound on a real player's.

## Current state

What works:
- `main` green (687 unit, 24 e2e incl. arc). This slice adds only an opt-in
  spec (confirmed skipped by default) and docs. tsc + eslint clean.

Read of the data:
- **Standard is loose.** Even the best case (diligent maintainer, optimal route)
  never strands, keeps the wagon mostly full (35 of 80 samples at 76+%, none at
  0), and ends ~1700 coins to the good after buying everything. This confirms the
  owner's prior by-feel read that standard "cruises."
- **Demanding bites.** It strands twice, spends most of the arc under 50%
  condition, and ends with roughly half the coins. So the selector's presets are
  meaningfully different, and the mechanic has real headroom.
- **The coin economy (#110) also reads loose:** on standard the player floats in
  ~1700 surplus coins after all upgrades and repairs, so repairs are not yet the
  recurring bite the sink was meant to create.
