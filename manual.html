Courier of the Borderlands

A courier drives the fractured frontier where roads are unreliable, settlements are isolated, and information matters as much as goods. You accept contracts, cross difficult ground, reveal the map, and build a name with the people who depend on you.

The delivery loop

The game turns on one loop. Do it well and everything else, the map, your name, your wagon, and the story, grows out of it.

  1. Accept a contract at a settlement's board.
  2. Drive to the pickup, if the cargo starts somewhere other than home.
  3. Carry it to the destination, choosing a route across the terrain.
  4. Deliver to collect coin and reputation, and to reveal more of the map on the way.
  5. Reinvest: repair the wagon, buy upgrades, spend skill points, and take the next contract.

Clear a region's standing contracts and its roads start to reconnect; keep going and the wider story of who is quietly rebuilding the borderland opens up.

Terrain and movement

The land is the main challenge. Roads are fast; leave them and you slow down and wear your wagon harder. Some ground you cannot cross at all until you earn the means to.

Fog of war

The map starts hidden. Driving pulls the fog back around you, so every delivery is also a scouting run. Reveal is tracked per region, so the ground you uncover stays uncovered when you return. A wider reveal radius (from the Wayfinder skill or lantern and chart upgrades) shows more of the road ahead and helps you spot shortcuts sooner.

Contracts and cargo

Contracts are offered on a settlement's board. Higher-value boards are gated behind reputation, and some contracts carry an optional bonus objective for extra pay. What you carry affects what you earn:

CargoPaysNotes
GoodsBaselineOrdinary cargo, paid at the reliable rate
Letters+5%Light and quick, worth a little more
Rumours+10%A whispered word is worth more than its weight
Secrets+20%Dangerous to carry, and it pays the most for a reason

Reputation and coin

Delivering earns coin and reputation with the settlement you serve. Reputation raises your standing tier, which unlocks better boards and improves your delivery rates. Coin is spent on repairs and upgrades.

TierReputation
Stranger0
Known3
Trusted8
Honoured15

The Negotiator skill adds to every delivery reward, and a reconnected road pays better than a broken one.

Wagon condition

Your wagon wears down as you travel, faster over rough ground than on the road. Watch the condition meter: let it run low and you risk being stranded at a crawl in the open. Repair at any settlement with R for coin, or, if you strand out on the road, pay for a tow home with R.

Repair is a real cost, not a formality. Plan a route and a budget that keep the wagon healthy, and repair before a long haul rather than limping home after one. The tank starts small and grows as you level.

Difficulty

Cycle the preset any time with G. It changes how fast the wagon wears and how much repair costs, so it sets how much the travel economy bites.

PresetFeel
RelaxedA bigger tank, gentler wear, cheaper repairs
StandardThe tuned default
DemandingA small tank, harsh wear, costly repairs; coin and upkeep matter constantly

Shortcuts and gated ground

Some routes are locked until you earn them, and they are usually the fast way through a region.

  • Fords start blocked. Reach a ford's signpost to open it as a shorter crossing. Each region has its own ford.
  • Deep mire opens once you hold the mire-crossing capability: the Marsh Treads upgrade, or Off-road rank 2.
  • Tidal flats open once you hold the tidal-crossing capability: the Salt Runners upgrade, or Off-road rank 3.

A longer dry route always exists, so a locked crossing is a shortcut to earn, never a dead end.

Story and the world

The story emerges from places and deliveries, not cutscenes. Talk to people with E, follow mission chains, and watch the world react as you reconnect its roads.

  • Dialogue and missions: settlements have people to talk to and mission chains that form the story spine.
  • Road encounters: brief, non-combat events on the road.
  • Discoveries: wayside lore you find by exploring; the Cipher skill decodes its deeper lines.
  • Weather: ambient conditions that nudge speed and visibility each run.

The regions

Three regions are open, linked by gateway tiles you cross with T when not carrying cargo. Each has its own map, settlements, contracts, ford, and fog.

  • Greybridge Region: the starting frontier and its bridge town.
  • Saltreach: a coastal region of tidal flats and salt marsh.
  • Fenmarch: deep fen country of mire and reed.

Tips

  • Stay on roads when you can; the time you save and the wear you avoid both pay off.
  • Repair before a long haul, not after. A stranded wagon costs a tow and a crawl home.
  • Chase reputation early to open better boards, then let Negotiator compound the pay.
  • Open a region's ford as soon as you can reach its signpost; it reshapes every later route.
  • Off-road is two things at once: raw speed, and the key to mire and tidal shortcuts. It earns its points.
  • On Demanding, treat coin as upkeep first and luxury second.
