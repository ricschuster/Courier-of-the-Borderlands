Converted to a focused-work equivalent it lands at roughly 4 to 7 days, well under even the P1 low end. That gap, not the raw line count, is the point of the page.

The lever

For a game like this the lever is not the tiles or the menus, which are comparatively mechanical once the stack is familiar. The effort that actually compounds sits in three places: making terrain-based movement feel fair and legible so the map reads at a glance, keeping content data-driven so a new region or contract is cheap to add rather than a rewrite, and getting save-state correctness right, where one subtle key change can orphan every existing save. Someone who has internalised those three moves finishes far faster than the line count suggests. Someone who has not spends most of their time there.

Reading this honestly

  • Measured versus estimated. The line counts, the 245 merged pull requests, the 85 test specs, and the 14 day window are read straight from the repository. The persona day figures and the focused-work equivalent of the AI-assisted build are judgment.
  • Same quality bar. Every estimate assumes the developer actually ships what shipped: strict TypeScript, unit and browser tests, CI, docs, three regions, and a live deploy. Skipping any of those moves the numbers down and the risk up.
  • Focused days, not calendar days. These are days of uninterrupted work. Built on evenings and weekends, stretch each range by two to three times before it maps to real calendar time.
  • P3 is open-ended on purpose. A new toolchain has a long, lumpy tail: the first browser deploy, the first strict-typing refactor, and the first hunt for a flaky end-to-end test each cost more than they look like they should.
