Courier of the Borderlands

A 2D fantasy courier game about delivering goods, letters, rumours, and secrets across a dangerous borderland, where every route reveals more of the map and changes your standing with the people who depend on you.

Play in your browser Read the manual

What you do

  1. Take a contract

    Pick up goods, letters, rumours, or secrets from a settlement board and load them onto your wagon.

  2. Cross the ground

    Plan a route over roads, mire, and tidal flats. Terrain sets your speed and grinds down your wagon.

  3. Get paid, grow

    Deliver for coin and reputation, then spend both on skills, upgrades, and better boards.

What makes it interesting

Exploration first

The map starts dark. Every run you take burns off more fog, and there is always one more stretch of road worth revealing.

Roads are the challenge

No combat. The difficulty is the ground itself: what it costs to cross, what it does to your wagon, and which way you should have gone.

A wagon that wears out

Condition drops as you travel rough ground. Run it to zero and you are stranded, paying for a tow and a long crawl home.

Reputation opens doors

Settlements remember you. Standing unlocks better contracts, and courier skills compound what each one pays.

Shortcuts you earn

Fords and tidal crossings stay shut until you have the capability to take them. Opening one reshapes every route after it.

Story through places

The world speaks through delivery notes, route names, wayside lore, and the people waiting at the far end.

A look at it

Grey-box art, for now. The systems come first; the frontier is still being drawn.

The frontier

Three regions are open, linked by gateway crossings. Each has its own map, settlements, contracts, and fog to burn off.

  • Greybridge Region: the starting frontier and its bridge town.
  • Saltreach: a coastal region of tidal flats and salt marsh.
  • Fenmarch: deep fen country of mire and reed.

Getting started

It runs in the browser, with nothing to install. Arrow keys or WASD to drive; the manual covers the rest, including the full controls reference and what each system is doing to you.
